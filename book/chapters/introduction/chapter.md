
# Introduction

At its core, ColdFusion is a straightforward, modern programming language and application server. You make a request to either a `.cfm` template or a `.cfc` (remote) component, ColdFusion intercepts and routes that request to the given file, and a response is rendered and returned to the client. And yet, within the context of that simple request/response life-cycle, there exist many caveats, quirks, curiosities, and confusions.

The goal of this book isn't to teach you ColdFusion — there are established resources for the basics; and the authors of this book assume that you have some foundational level of understanding about how ColdFusion works. The goal of this book is to go beyond the initial simplicity and surface those aspects of the language that may not be obvious.

Thar be dragons. And we hope to be your dragon slayers.
